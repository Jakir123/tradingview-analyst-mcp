# Jesse Livermore — Trend Following & Trade Management
**Best for:** Trade management rules, position sizing, knowing when to hold vs sell

## The Core Idea
Livermore made and lost several fortunes. What he learned — and what works forever — is about following the trend, managing losses ruthlessly, and letting winners run without emotion. These are not entry strategies but *rules of conduct* to apply on top of any strategy.

---

## The Livermore Trend Rules

### Rule 1: Trade With the Line of Least Resistance
- If the market is going up, only buy. If it is going down, only sell or stay out.
- Never fight the primary trend of the market (DSEX index direction)
- *"The trend is your friend until it ends."*

**How to apply on DSE:**
- Check DSEBD:DSEX weekly chart first
- If DSEX is below its 20-week MA → only trade with extreme selectivity or stay in cash
- If DSEX is above its 20-week MA and rising → be aggressive with entries

### Rule 2: Never Average Down on a Losing Position
- If you buy a stock and it goes down, DO NOT BUY MORE
- Averaging down is the #1 way retail traders destroy their accounts
- A stock going against you is telling you that you were wrong — listen to it
- *"The only thing to do when a man is wrong is to be right — by ceasing to be wrong."*

**DSE trap:** Many DSE investors "average down" as stocks fall from 100 to 50 to 20. This is fatal.

### Rule 3: Pyramid Into Winners, Not Losers
- When a stock goes UP after you buy, add more to the position
- Add smaller amounts each time (e.g., buy 100 shares, add 50 on first move up, add 25 more)
- Never add more than the original position size in follow-on buys
- *"Only buy a stock that is going up. If it is not going up, why own it?"*

### Rule 4: Cut Every Loss at a Predetermined Point
- Before entering any trade, know EXACTLY where you will exit if wrong
- Write down your stop loss before entering
- When the stop is hit, EXIT IMMEDIATELY — no hoping, no waiting
- Maximum loss per trade: 7-10% of entry price
- *"I have always made my money by sitting, not by thinking."*

### Rule 5: Let Winners Run — Don't Sell Too Early
- The biggest mistake: selling a winner too early because you're afraid of giving back gains
- Hold winners as long as they are above their key moving average (10-week MA)
- Livermore's rule: never sell a stock just because the price has gone up
- Sell only when: (a) the trend breaks, (b) it hits a climax run, (c) fundamentals change

### Rule 6: Time the Entry — Wait for the Pivotal Point
- Every big move starts from a pivotal point — a key breakout level
- Do NOT buy in the middle of a range — wait for the stock to prove itself
- Livermore called this the "Pivotal Point": the exact price where a stock's direction is confirmed
- **Pivotal point on DSE = the breakout above a box top or resistance level**

### Rule 7: Only Trade What You Understand
- Never buy a stock because someone told you to
- Never buy because it "looks cheap"
- Understand WHY the stock should go up before risking money

## Livermore's Position Sizing Rules

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| New trade, unproven | 25% of intended position size |
| Trade confirms (moves in your favor) | Add next 25% |
| Trade continues to confirm | Add remaining 50% |
| Trade goes against you from start | EXIT the entire position |
| Already have a loss | Do NOT add |

## Livermore's Market Timing Rules

### When to Be Aggressive (Fully Invested)
- Market (DSEX) is in a clear uptrend
- Most sectors are participating
- Volume on up days > volume on down days in the market
- New 52-week highs are expanding

### When to Step Back (Reduce Exposure)
- Market (DSEX) makes a new high but volume is declining
- Leading stocks start to break down
- Distribution days (high volume market drops) are appearing
- 3+ distribution days in 2 weeks = warning sign

### When to Go to Cash (Protect Capital)
- Market breaks below its 20-week moving average
- More than 50% of your positions are at a loss
- Broad market is in a downtrend (Stage 4)
- You don't understand what is moving the market

## The Livermore Journal Method

Livermore kept a detailed trading journal. For every trade, record:
1. **Why I entered** — what was the setup?
2. **Entry price and stop loss**
3. **What actually happened**
4. **What I learned**

Reviewing this journal regularly is how he identified which setups worked for him.

**Ask Claude to help:** *"Review my last 10 trades and identify patterns in my winners vs my losers."*

## Trade Management Checklist (Use Before Every Trade)

- [ ] Is the DSEX index in an uptrend? (check weekly chart)
- [ ] Is this stock in Stage 2? (Weinstein)
- [ ] Does it pass the SEPA checklist? (Minervini)
- [ ] Is there a clear entry point (box breakout / VCP pivot)?
- [ ] Is my stop loss level defined BEFORE entry?
- [ ] Is my risk less than 2% of my total portfolio?
- [ ] Is my reward at least 2x my risk?
- [ ] Am I buying because of a setup, not because of emotion or a tip?
