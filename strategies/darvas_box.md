# Nicolas Darvas — Box Theory
**Best for:** Swing trade breakout entries, identifying consolidation before big moves

## The Core Idea
Stocks move in a series of "boxes" — periods of consolidation between price advances. When a stock breaks out of a box with high volume, it enters the next box at a higher level. Buy the breakout, place your stop at the bottom of the box, ride to the next box.

Darvas turned $36,000 into $2.25 million in 18 months using only this method — while traveling the world as a professional dancer, checking prices by telegram.

---

## What is a Box?

A box is a tight consolidation range where:
- The stock makes a HIGH (top of box) and does not meaningfully exceed it for several days
- The stock makes a LOW (bottom of box) and does not meaningfully break below it
- Price bounces between these two levels repeatedly
- Volume is LOW and shrinking during the box (nobody selling, nobody buying aggressively)

```
Box Top:    ────────────────────────
            |  price bounces here  |
            |  ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~|
            |  volume dries up     |
Box Bottom: ────────────────────────
```

---

## How to Draw a Box (Step by Step)

1. Find a recent high that held for at least **3 consecutive days** without being exceeded → this is the **box top**
2. Find the corresponding low during that same period → this is the **box bottom**
3. The range between top and bottom should be **no more than 15-20%** (tighter = better)
4. Volume should be **declining** while price is inside the box

---

## The Buy Signal

**Buy when:** Price closes ABOVE the box top with volume SURGING (at least 1.5-2x average)

- This means smart money is entering aggressively
- The stock is breaking out of its consolidation
- A new, higher box is about to form

**Entry:** Place a buy order just above the box top (0.5-1% above)
- On DSE: Use a limit order since circuit breakers can limit gap-ups

---

## Stop Loss Placement

- Stop loss goes **just below the bottom of the box** you just broke out of
- If the stock falls back into the box after breaking out, the setup has failed — exit
- **Darvas rule:** *"Never let a loss exceed the height of the box"*
- Typical stop: 5-10% below entry (depending on box size)

---

## Profit Target

- **Minimum target:** Height of the box × 2 projected above the breakout
- Example: Box from 100 to 110 (height = 10 BDT) → target = 120 BDT
- **Better approach:** Hold until the stock forms the NEXT box at a higher level, then raise your stop to the bottom of the new box

---

## Multiple Box Stacking (How Big Moves Work)

```
Box 3:  [260─────280]  ← current box forming
         ↑ breakout
Box 2:  [240─────260]  ← held here, then broke out
         ↑ breakout
Box 1:  [220─────240]  ← original base
```

- Each breakout leads to a new higher box
- Keep raising your stop to the bottom of the most recent box
- Ride the stock up through multiple boxes for maximum gains

---

## Box Identification Rules (Strict)

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Box duration | Minimum 1 week, ideally 2-4 weeks |
| Box width | Maximum 15-20% range (tighter = higher probability) |
| Volume in box | Must be declining or quiet |
| Volume on breakout | Must be 1.5x+ above average |
| False breakout filter | Price must CLOSE above box top, not just touch it intraday |

## Darvas Box on DSE — Practical Guide

**Best stocks for Darvas boxes on DSE:**
- Mid-to-large cap stocks with avg volume > 80,000/day
- Stocks that have recently hit new 52-week highs (new highs = new boxes)
- Sectors that are in market favour (telecom, pharma, banking)

**Common DSE box patterns:**
- Post-earnings consolidation → powerful breakouts
- Post-dividend consolidation → reliable boxes
- 3-6 week tight ranges → strongest setups

**How to ask Claude to find boxes:**
> "Look at the last 60 bars. Identify any box formation — a tight range where price consolidated for at least 5 days. Find the box top, box bottom, and tell me if we are near a breakout."

---

## Red Flags — When NOT to Use Darvas Box

- Volume is high INSIDE the box (distribution happening)
- Box is too wide (>20%) — not a clean setup
- Stock is in a Stage 4 downtrend (never buy downtrend boxes)
- Market (DSEX index) is in a downtrend — avoid breakouts in bear markets
- Stock has already broken out and is 10%+ above the box — too late, wait for next box
